U.S. Solar Panels and Daily Sunlight

Authored by: Calvin Horning

This map was made using the features of QGIS 4.10.11. The solar panel data came from the U.S. Large-Scale Solar Photovoltaic Database and was joined to a 50km minor-axis hexbin (made using the MMQGIS "Create Grid Layer" tool) with the Join Attributes by location (summary) tool. State and county data came from the U.S. Census Bureau. The daily sunlight data is from the North America Land Data Assimilation System via CDC WONDER, and was appended to the county layer via the "join" tool and a CSV layer without geometry. This map is intended to show the density of solar panel farms in the continental U.S. as compared with the regions with the greatest amounts of sunlight. In making this map, I expected high-sunlight and low density areas to have the highest rate of solar panels. You'll see that though there is some correlation, much of the relatively dense and lower-sunlight East Coast have high rates of solar farming. This leads me to wonder what political economic factors effect large-scale solar panel construction beyond physical geography.
